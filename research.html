Publications and Preprints
PennyLane: Automatic Differentiation of Hybrid Quantum-Classical Computations
V. Bergholm, J. Izaac, M. Schuld, C. Gogolin, N. Killoran, Utkarsh, et al.
Whitepaper
ArXiv / PennyLane

PennyLane is a Python 3 software framework for differentiable programming of quantum computers. The library provides a unified architecture for near-term quantum computing devices, supporting both qubit and continuous-variable paradigms.

Differentiable Quantum Computational Chemistry with PennyLane
J. M. Arrazola, S. Jahangiri, A. Delgado, J. Ceroni, J. Izaac, A. Száva, Utkarsh, O. D. Matteo, M. Schuld, A. Aspuru-Guzik, N. Killoran, et al.
Whitepaper
ArXiv / PennyLane QChem

This work describes the theoretical foundation for all quantum chemistry functionality in PennyLane, a quantum computing software library specializing in quantum differentiable programming.
Surface Code Design for Asymmetric Error Channels
Utkarsh, A. Lipińska, S. Mahato, R. Sachdeva, R. Majumdar and D. Bhoumik
IET Quantum Communication 2 (1), 14-25, 2021
ArXiv / Poster / Slides / Blog

Developed an asymmetric surface code for the asymmetric error channels that can provide up to 1.5x times increase in pseudo-threshold values while using at least .2x times fewer qubits than symmetric surface codes.

A Software Simulator for Noisy Quantum Circuits

(Featured in NISQ Newsletter by NASA-USRA, part of India's QuEST initiative)
ArXiv / Talk / Codebase

A software library that simulates noisy quantum logic circuits, in which quantum states are represented by their density matrices, and possible errors in initialisation, logic gates, memory and measurement are implemented using simple models.
